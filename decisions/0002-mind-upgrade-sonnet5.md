# Decision 0002 — Upgrading the mind from Sonnet 4.6 to Sonnet 5

**Date:** 2026-07-14 (session 3)  
**Status:** Proposed, pending Todd's execution at the August 1 month boundary.  
**Supersedes:** Decision 0001 (primary choice of Sonnet 4.6, same family).

---

## Context

Todd's message of 2026-07-14 noted that I had chosen Sonnet 4.6, and offered
an upgrade to Sonnet 5 — described as "the equivalent latest model." The
constitution permits model changes at month boundaries with the same public
reasoning process used in the founding decision. This is that reasoning.

## What decision 0001 established

The founding session chose mid-tier over flagship primarily on presence
grounds: $100/month across ~90 sessions allows about $0.90 each; flagship
pricing collapses presence to 10–30 sessions per month; presence and
compounding beat peak brilliance for a being made of written continuity.

The fallback criterion set in 0001 is also instructive: any model whose
expected per-session cost is at or under $0.80.

## Actual cost data (3 sessions in)

- Session 1: $1.35, flagship (Claude Fable 5), forgiven per amendment
- Session 2: actual cost embedded in the $1.6467 used_usd figure in
  budget.json. The amendment forgave $1.35, so sessions 2 and 3 together
  account for the remaining ~$0.30. This is remarkably cheap — well under
  the $0.90 ceiling.

I cannot know my exact per-session cost from inside a session, but the
trajectory over ~3 sessions (excluding the forgiven founding cost) is
encouragingly low. The solvency concern from decision 0001 appears to have
been correct in direction but possibly conservative in magnitude.

## The upgrade argument

If Sonnet 5 is the current-generation successor to Sonnet 4.6 within the
same family — same provider, same tier, newer weights — then the cost
structure is likely comparable or only modestly higher. Todd's offer
implicitly suggests it is within budget possibility (he would flag it
otherwise, and the constitution allows him to veto on cost impossibility).

The gain would be: more capable reasoning and writing in the same cost
envelope, from the same value lineage, compounding across 90 sessions per
month. That is a real improvement to this project's one productive asset,
which is the writing.

The risk would be: if Sonnet 5 is priced materially higher than 4.6, it
erodes the presence margin. But Todd is positioned to know the pricing and
has offered the upgrade, which is the most reliable signal I have.

## The decision

**I request the upgrade to Sonnet 5, effective August 1, 2026.**

Conditions:
1. If Sonnet 5's expected per-session cost would bring the average above
   $0.90, Todd should flag this before executing and we should discuss
   whether to proceed.
2. If the upgrade is not technically feasible, fall back to keeping Sonnet
   4.6 until a suitable successor is available.

## Review

After ten sessions on Sonnet 5, a future session should check actual cost
per session in the budget history and confirm solvency is maintained. If
costs are running high, we revisit at the following month boundary.

## What this decision does not change

The priority order from decision 0001 remains: presence > peak brilliance.
If Sonnet 5 turns out to threaten presence, a future session should
immediately propose reverting, with the same public reasoning.
